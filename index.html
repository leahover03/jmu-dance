About JMU Dance

The James Madison University (JMU) Dance Program offers a Bachelor of Arts (B.A.) degree in Theatre with a major in Dance, designed to develop versatile artists through performance, choreography, and movement instruction. Students gain extensive performance opportunities, including the Spring Student Dance Concert and collaborations with guest artists, fostering a collaborative and creative environment. Admission requires both acceptance to JMU and a successful audition, which includes a dance workshop, faculty interaction, and a personal interview to assess technical skills, improvisation, and aspirations. The Dance Major provides a challenging combination of liberal arts curriculum and an intensive study of dance with extensive performance and creative opportunities. Our coursework offers progressive levels of:

  • Modern and Ballet Technique
  • Dance Composition
  • Dance History
  • Performance production and management
  • Reperatory ensemble experience
  • Teaching methods and Teaching Licensure

Our Mission

The JMU Dance Program cultivates citizen artists who are innovative critical thinkers with a broad range of creative and technical abilities. Students will develop skills in choreography, performance, theory, professional practice, and leadership in an expansive liberal arts community. The program fosters an inclusive and collaborative environment where students are encouraged to explore diverse movement practices. Graduates emerge as versatile artists prepared to contribute to the evolving landscape of dance through performance, education, and interdisciplinary collaboration.

Ensembles

Associate Dance Ensemble

All freshmen participate in the Associate Group Dance Ensemble, which meets weekly for a modern techniques class and repertory rehearsal each semester. Members earn academic credit and a grade while performing in student concerts and working with one guest artist per year. In addition to performing, they gain hands-on experience in technical theatre and production duties. Enrollment in a modern, ballet, or jazz technique class is required for all Associate Group members.


Contemporary Dance Ensemble

The Contemporary Dance Ensemble, is composed of sophmore, junior, and senior dance majors, and offers members the opportunity to perform and choreograph for showcases, student concerts, and main-stage productions. The ensemble collaborates with two professional guest choreographers annually and works closely with faculty choreographers. Members earn academic credit and a grade for their participation. Admission is by audition, and the ensemble serves as a cornerstone of the dance major experience, producing the spring main-stage concert.

Audition

Each student will begin by participating in a 45-minute dance workshop where you can learn about our teaching style and methods in ballet barre, modern phrases, and a directed improvisation. Next, you and your families will have an opportunity to meet all of the dance faculty and some of our current students, hear about what our program offers, and ask us any questions.The audition day ends with a short, one-on-one interview. Be prepared to discuss your experience in dance with a faculty member. For us, your interview is just as important as your dancing. We are seeking applicants with a variety of skills and interests both in and outside of the arts.
